Anatomy of a perfect copywriting prompt

Every effective copywriting prompt includes these essential elements:

1. Role/Persona

Define who the AI should be: "You are an expert copywriter specializing in direct response marketing..."

2. Context

Provide background: "We're selling a productivity app to busy professionals..."

3. Task

Be specific: "Write a 300-word sales page headline and subheadline..."

4. Format

Specify structure: "Use the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) framework..."

5. Tone/Voice

Set the style: "Write in a conversational, urgent tone..."

6. Constraints

Set limits: "Keep it under 50 words, avoid jargon..."

⭐ Pro Tip: The more specific your prompt, the better the output. Instead of "write an email," try "write a 200-word promotional email for a SaaS product targeting small business owners, using a friendly but professional tone, with a clear CTA to start a free trial."

Email Copywriting Prompts

Welcome Email Series

📧 Welcome Email #1

Prompt: "You are an email marketing expert. Write a warm, engaging welcome email for new subscribers to [YOUR BRAND]. Include: 1) Thank them for joining, 2) Set expectations for what they'll receive, 3) Share one quick win or valuable tip they can use immediately, 4) Include a soft CTA to follow us on [SOCIAL PLATFORM]. Keep it under 200 words, conversational tone."

Perfect for: Building relationships

Promotional Email

📧 Product Launch Email

Prompt: "Write a persuasive promotional email for [PRODUCT NAME] launching on [DATE]. Target audience: [DESCRIBE AUDIENCE]. Use the AIDA framework (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action). Include: compelling subject line, opening hook, 3 key benefits with specifics, social proof element, urgent CTA with deadline. Tone: excited but professional. Length: 250-300 words."

Perfect for: Product launches

Abandoned Cart Email

📧 Cart Recovery Email

Prompt: "Write an abandoned cart recovery email for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Use urgency and FOMO. Include: 1) Reminder of what they left behind, 2) Benefit-focused reminder, 3) Limited-time incentive (discount/free shipping), 4) Clear CTA button text. Keep it short (150 words max), friendly but urgent tone. Add PS with scarcity element."

Perfect for: E-commerce recovery

Nurture Email

📧 Value-First Nurture Email

Prompt: "Write a nurture email that provides value before selling. Topic: [YOUR TOPIC]. Structure: 1) Relatable opening about [PAIN POINT], 2) Share one actionable tip or insight, 3) Brief mention of how [YOUR SOLUTION] helps, 4) Soft CTA to learn more. Tone: helpful, not salesy. Length: 200-250 words."

Perfect for: Building trust

Sales Page & Landing Page Prompts

Hero Section

🎯 Headline & Subheadline

Prompt: "Write 5 headline variations for a landing page selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE] to [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Each headline should: 1) Address their main pain point, 2) Promise a specific benefit, 3) Be under 12 words. Then write 3 subheadlines (under 20 words each) that expand on the promise and add credibility. Use power words and emotional triggers."

Perfect for: Above-the-fold copy

Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)

🎯 PAS Framework Section

Prompt: "Write a PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) section for [PRODUCT]. Problem: Describe the struggle [AUDIENCE] faces with [ISSUE]. Agitate: Amplify the pain - what happens if they don't solve this? Use emotional language. Solution: Introduce [PRODUCT] as the answer. Highlight 3 key features that directly solve the problem. Total: 300-400 words. Tone: empathetic but confident."

Perfect for: Main sales copy

Benefits Section

🎯 Benefit Bullets

Prompt: "Write 7 compelling benefit bullets for [PRODUCT]. Each bullet should: 1) Start with a strong verb, 2) Focus on outcome not feature, 3) Include specific numbers or timeframes where possible, 4) Address an objection or fear. Format: '✓ [BENEFIT] so you can [DESIRED OUTCOME] without [COMMON OBSTACLE].' Keep each under 15 words."

Perfect for: Feature lists

Social Proof

🎯 Testimonial Section

Prompt: "Write 3 testimonial-style quotes for [PRODUCT] from different customer personas: 1) [PERSONA 1 - e.g., busy mom], 2) [PERSONA 2 - e.g., entrepreneur], 3) [PERSONA 3 - e.g., corporate professional]. Each testimonial should: mention specific results, address a common objection, feel authentic and conversational. Include name and title for each. 40-60 words each."

Perfect for: Building credibility

Ad Copy Prompts

Facebook/Instagram Ads

📱 FB Ad Primary Text

Prompt: "Write 3 Facebook ad primary text variations for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Target: [AUDIENCE]. Use the hook-story-offer framework. Hook: Grab attention in first sentence. Story: Relatable scenario or statistic. Offer: Clear value proposition. Include emojis strategically (2-3 max). Add a clear CTA. Keep each under 125 characters for mobile optimization. Tone: [SPECIFY TONE]."

Perfect for: Social ads

Google Ads

🔍 Google Search Ad

Prompt: "Write 5 Google Search ad headlines (30 characters max each) and 3 descriptions (90 characters max) for [KEYWORD/PRODUCT]. Include: 1) Primary keyword in headline, 2) Unique selling proposition, 3) Call-to-action, 4) Urgency or scarcity element, 5) Specific benefit or number. Make them compelling and click-worthy."

Perfect for: PPC campaigns

Social Media Copy Prompts

Instagram Caption

📸 Instagram Post Caption

Prompt: "Write an engaging Instagram caption for [TOPIC/PRODUCT]. Structure: 1) Hook in first line (emoji + intriguing statement), 2) 2-3 sentences of value/story, 3) Question to drive comments, 4) 5-7 relevant hashtags. Tone: [SPECIFY - casual/professional/funny]. Keep main caption under 150 words. Include line breaks for readability."

Perfect for: Instagram engagement

LinkedIn Post

💼 LinkedIn Thought Leadership

Prompt: "Write a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC/INDUSTRY INSIGHT]. Use this structure: 1) Bold opening statement or question, 2) Share a lesson learned or mistake made, 3) Provide 3 actionable takeaways, 4) End with thought-provoking question. Professional but conversational tone. 200-250 words. Include relevant emojis (3-4 max). Add '👇' before the question."

Perfect for: B2B engagement

Advanced Prompt Engineering Techniques

1. Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Ask AI to think step-by-step:

Example: "Before writing the copy, analyze: 1) Who is the target audience and what are their pain points? 2) What are the top 3 objections they might have? 3) What emotional triggers will resonate most? Then write the sales page using this analysis."

2. Few-Shot Learning

Provide examples:

Example: "Write product descriptions in this style: [EXAMPLE 1], [EXAMPLE 2]. Now write one for [YOUR PRODUCT] following the same tone, structure, and length."

3. Role-Playing

Assign specific expertise:

Example: "You are David Ogilvy, legendary copywriter. Write a headline and opening paragraph for [PRODUCT] using your principles of specificity, benefits, and curiosity."

4. Constraint-Based Prompting

Set specific limits:

Example: "Write a tweet thread (5 tweets max, 280 characters each) about [TOPIC]. Each tweet must: end with a hook for the next, include one statistic or fact, use 1 emoji max, no hashtags until final tweet."

How to customize prompts for your brand

Make these prompts work for YOUR brand:

1. Define Your Brand Voice

  • Create a brand voice document with adjectives (friendly, authoritative, playful, etc.)
  • Include 3-5 example sentences that represent your brand
  • Add this to every prompt: "Write in a [ADJECTIVE] tone. Example of our voice: [EXAMPLE]"

2. Build a Prompt Library

  • Save prompts that work well in a document or Notion database
  • Note which variations performed best
  • Create templates with [BRACKETS] for easy customization

3. Iterate and Improve

  • Test different prompt variations
  • Track which prompts generate the best conversions
  • Refine based on results

💡 Remember: AI is a tool to amplify your creativity, not replace it. Always review, edit, and add your unique perspective to AI-generated copy. The best copy combines AI efficiency with human insight.

Final tips for AI copywriting success

Follow these best practices:

  • Be Specific: Vague prompts = vague results
  • Provide Context: The more background, the better
  • Iterate: First draft is rarely the best draft
  • Test: A/B test AI-generated copy against human-written
  • Edit Ruthlessly: AI is a starting point, not the finish line
  • Add Personality: Inject your brand's unique voice
