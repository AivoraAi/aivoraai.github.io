Step-by-Step: Removing Noise from Podcast Audio

Ready to clean up your podcast audio? Here's my proven workflow using Adobe Podcast Enhance (the most popular choice):

Step 1: Record Your Podcast

Record as usual, even with background noise present. Tips for best AI results:

  • Use the best microphone available — USB or XLR mic (not laptop mic)
  • Get close to the mic — 6-12 inches from your mouth
  • Record in WAV format — Higher quality than MP3
  • Minimize noise at source — Close windows, turn off fans if possible
  • Record a few seconds of silence — Helps AI identify background noise

Step 2: Export Your Audio File

Export your recording in a compatible format:

  • WAV — Best quality, larger file size (recommended)
  • MP3 — Smaller file size, good quality
  • M4A — Apple format, also works well
  • Maximum file size: 500MB for free Adobe account

Step 3: Upload to Adobe Podcast Enhance

Go to podcast.adobe.com/enhance:

  1. Sign in with free Adobe account (or create one)
  2. Click "Upload Audio"
  3. Select your podcast file
  4. Wait for upload (1-5 minutes depending on file size)

Step 4: Process the Audio

Once uploaded:

  1. Click "Enhance" button
  2. Wait 1-3 minutes for AI processing
  3. Preview the enhanced audio
  4. Compare before/after using the toggle
  5. Adjust intensity if needed (default is usually best)

Step 5: Download Clean Audio

When satisfied with results:

  1. Click "Download"
  2. Choose format (WAV for best quality, MP3 for smaller size)
  3. Save to your computer
  4. Import into your editing software or publish directly

⚠️ Important: Always keep your original recording! Save the AI-enhanced version as a separate file. This way, you can always go back if needed.

Optimizing Results: Pro Tips

Get the best possible results from AI noise removal:

Recording Best Practices

  • Use a pop filter — Reduces plosives (p, b sounds)
  • Treat your room — Even blankets on walls reduce echo
  • Record at proper levels — Aim for -12dB to -6dB peak
  • Use headphones — Monitor audio while recording
  • Record room tone — 10 seconds of silence helps AI

AI Processing Tips

  • Don't over-process — Moderate settings sound more natural
  • Preview before exporting — Always listen to full result
  • Combine tools — Use Krisp for recording + Adobe for final polish
  • Check for artifacts — Listen for robotic or metallic sounds
  • Export in high quality — WAV or 320kbps MP3 minimum
