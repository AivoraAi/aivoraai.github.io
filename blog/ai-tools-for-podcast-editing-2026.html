How to Use AI Tools for Podcast Editing: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Record with Quality in Mind

Use Riverside or Zencastr for remote interviews to capture local, high-quality audio tracks. For solo recordings, use a decent microphone in a quiet space. Even the best AI can't fully rescue terrible source audio.

Step 2: Enhance Audio with Adobe Podcast

Upload your raw audio files to Adobe Podcast Enhance. The AI will automatically remove background noise, reduce echo, and enhance voice clarity. This step alone can transform home recordings into studio-quality audio.

Step 3: Edit with Descript

Import your enhanced audio into Descript. The AI will automatically transcribe everything. Edit by deleting text in the transcript—remove filler words, mistakes, and dead air. Use the "Remove Filler Words" feature for one-click cleanup of "um," "uh," and "like."

Step 4: Level & Export

Use Auphonic or Descript's built-in tools to normalize audio levels across your episode. Export in your preferred format (MP3 for most platforms, WAV for highest quality). Most AI tools can also auto-generate show notes and chapter markers.
