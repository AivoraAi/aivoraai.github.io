Building an Async-First Workflow

Step 1: Automate Note-Taking

Connect Otter.ai or Zoom AI Companion to your calendar. Stop manually taking notes. Let the AI record, transcribe, and summarize meetings. Share the link with team members who couldn't attend.

Step 2: Shift to Video Updates

Instead of a status meeting, record a 3-minute Loom video updating the team on your progress. Use Loom AI to generate a summary text for those who prefer reading over watching.

Step 3: Centralize Knowledge

Push all summaries and docs into Notion. Use Notion AI to query your database ("What did we decide about the marketing budget last week?"). This prevents information silos.

Step 4: Protect Focus Time

Use Clockwise to block "Focus Time" on your calendar. It automatically moves less important meetings to ensure you have deep work hours every day.

Frequently Asked Questions

The "Big Three" for remote work are Otter.ai (for meetings), Notion AI (for docs), and Loom (for video). These three cover 90% of remote communication needs.

AI scheduling tools like Clockwise and Calendly analyze all participants' time zones and calendars to find the only slot where everyone is available and awake, reducing scheduling ping-pong.

Most enterprise-grade tools (Zoom, Notion, Otter) are SOC2 compliant. However, when using generative AI, be careful not to paste sensitive PII (Personally Identifiable Information) or proprietary secrets unless the tool is approved by your company security policy.
