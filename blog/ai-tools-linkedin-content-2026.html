How to Use AI Tools for LinkedIn Content: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Generate Post Ideas with ChatGPT or Taplio

Open ChatGPT and use this prompt: "Generate 5 LinkedIn post ideas for a [your industry] professional targeting [your audience]. Focus on topics that drive engagement: personal stories, contrarian opinions, how-to guides, and industry predictions. Include a hook for each."

Review the ideas, pick the strongest one, and move to writing. This alone saves 20-30 minutes of brainstorming.

Step 2: Write the Full Post with ChatGPT or Jasper

Use this advanced prompt: "Write a LinkedIn post about [topic]. Hook: [your hook]. Structure: Hook → Personal story/insight → 3 actionable takeaways → Question for engagement. Tone: conversational but professional. Length: 150-200 words. Include 3 relevant hashtags."

Edit the output to add your personal voice, specific experiences, and authentic opinions. Never publish raw AI output. If you need help crafting the perfect prompt, check out our guide on the best AI prompt generators.

Step 3: Create Visuals with Canva AI or Gamma

For standard posts: Use Canva AI, search "LinkedIn" in templates, select a post or carousel template, and use Magic Replace to add your branding. For document carousels: Use Gamma.app, enter your post topic, and let AI generate a 5-10 slide carousel with professional layouts.

Step 4: Schedule with Buffer for Optimal Timing

Connect your LinkedIn to Buffer. Schedule your posts for Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM or 12-1 PM in your audience's timezone. Buffer's AI will suggest adjustments based on your historical engagement data.

Step 5: Engage with AI-Assisted Comments

Use ChatGPT to draft thoughtful comments on posts in your niche. Prompt: "Write a thoughtful, value-adding comment on a LinkedIn post about [topic]. Add a genuine insight or question. Keep it under 50 words." This helps you stay active and visible without spending hours scrolling.

How We Tested These Tools

Over 30 days (April 20 - May 20, 2026), our team tested each tool against real LinkedIn content tasks:

  • Post writing: Each tool was used to write 10 LinkedIn posts across different formats (text, carousel, video script)
  • Time tracking: We measured time from prompt to publish-ready draft
  • Quality scoring: Posts were rated by 3 independent reviewers on engagement potential, authenticity, and professionalism (1-5 scale)
  • Engagement testing: A subset of AI-assisted posts were published to test real-world engagement vs. manually written posts
  • Feature depth: We evaluated unique features, integrations, and workflow fit for LinkedIn specifically

All tools were tested with free tiers where available. Paid features were tested during free trials. No tool manufacturer influenced our ratings or rankings.
