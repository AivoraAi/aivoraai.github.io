My Exact Workflow: Zoom to LinkedIn in 20 Minutes

Here's the step-by-step process I use to turn every Zoom call into LinkedIn content:

Record & Upload

Enable Zoom cloud recording. After the call ends, OpusClip automatically detects new recordings via Zapier integration. Alternatively, manually upload the MP4 file.

Time: 2 minutes

AI Analysis

The AI scans your video for:

  • High-engagement moments (laughter, emphasis, key statements)
  • Complete thoughts that work as standalone clips
  • Visual variety (face changes, screen shares, reactions)

Time: 5-8 minutes (automated)

Review & Select

OpusClip generates 10-15 clips with virality scores. I review and select the top 5-7. The AI is surprisingly accurate—I approve 80% of suggestions.

Time: 5 minutes

Customize Captions

I tweak captions for clarity and add my brand colors. OpusClip's auto-captions are 95% accurate, but I fix industry-specific terms.

Time: 3 minutes per clip

Export & Schedule

Download clips in LinkedIn-optimized format (1080x1080 or 1080x1920). I use Buffer to schedule 1 clip per day for the next week.

Time: 5 minutes

✅ Total Time: 20 minutes for 5-7 LinkedIn posts. That's 4 minutes per post vs 2-3 hours manually.

Best Practices for LinkedIn Video Success

Just because AI makes it easy doesn't mean you should skip strategy. Here's what actually works:

⏱️ Length

30-90 Seconds

LinkedIn's algorithm favors this range. Short enough to maintain attention, long enough to deliver value.

📱 Format

Square or Vertical

1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1920 (vertical). Takes up more mobile screen real estate.

🎯 Hook

First 3 Seconds

Start with the most compelling statement. No "Hey everyone, today I'll..." intros.

📝 Description

150-200 Words

Add context, ask questions, include 3-5 relevant hashtags.

Don't just post random clips. Group related content into themes:

  • Monday: Industry insights
  • Wednesday: Client case studies
  • Friday: Behind-the-scenes

2. Repurpose Across Platforms

One Zoom call = content for:

  • LinkedIn (short clips)
  • YouTube (full webinar)
  • Twitter/X (quote graphics)
  • Blog post (transcript)
  • Newsletter (key takeaways)

3. Use AI to Write Captions

Don't just rely on auto-generated video captions. Use AI to write compelling post descriptions too. I use ChatGPT with this prompt:

# LinkedIn Post Description Prompt "Write a LinkedIn post description for a video clip about [topic]. Include: - Hook in first line - 3 key takeaways - Question to drive comments - 3-5 relevant hashtags Tone: Professional but conversational Length: 150-200 words"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Posting without captions — You'll lose 85% of viewers
  • Making clips too long — Over 2 minutes = drop-off
  • Ignoring the hook — First 3 seconds determine success
  • Posting inconsistently — Algorithm favors regular posters
  • No call-to-action — Tell viewers what to do next

✅ What TO Do

  • Test different formats — Try square, vertical, horizontal
  • Engage with comments — Reply within 2 hours
  • Use analytics — Double down on what works
  • Add branding — Consistent colors, fonts, logo
  • Batch create — Make 10 clips in one session
