🚀 Detailed Execution Guide

1️⃣
Step 1: Prepare Your Documents

Gather all contracts you need to review. Ensure they're in PDF format (most AI tools prefer PDFs). If you have scanned documents, use OCR software first to make them searchable. For large volumes, organize contracts by type (NDAs, employment agreements, vendor contracts) for easier batch processing.

2️⃣
Step 2: Choose Your Extraction Fields

Decide what data you need to extract. Common fields include: Party names, Effective dates, Termination dates, Payment terms and amounts, Liability caps, Governing law, Confidentiality obligations, Intellectual property rights, Dispute resolution mechanisms. Most tools allow you to create custom extraction templates.

3️⃣
Step 3: Upload and Process

Upload your documents to the AI platform. For tools like Harvey AI or Kira Systems, this might take a few minutes per document. For ChatPDF, processing is nearly instant. The AI will analyze the document structure, identify legal clauses, and extract the data points you specified.

4️⃣
Step 4: Review the AI Output

This is critical: always review AI-generated summaries and extracted data. While AI is highly accurate, it can miss nuances or misinterpret ambiguous language. Look for: Missing clauses, Incorrect date extractions, Misidentified parties, Unusual terms that need human review. The AI is your assistant, not your replacement.

5️⃣
Step 5: Export and Integrate

Export the extracted data in your preferred format (Excel, CSV, Word, JSON). Many tools integrate directly with contract management systems like DocuSign CLM, Icertis, or custom databases. Set up automated workflows to route flagged contracts to senior attorneys for final review.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

I've seen many legal professionals struggle with AI contract analysis. Here are the pitfalls to avoid:

❌ Critical Mistakes

  • Blindly trusting AI output without human review
  • Uploading confidential documents to unsecured platforms
  • Not verifying AI-extracted dates and monetary amounts
  • Using general-purpose AI instead of legal-specific tools
  • Ignoring jurisdiction-specific legal nuances
  • Not training the AI on your firm's specific contract templates
  • Skipping the verification step to "save time"

✅ Best Practices

  • Always have a qualified attorney review AI summaries
  • Use enterprise-grade tools with SOC 2 compliance and encryption
  • Double-check all extracted dates, amounts, and party names
  • Choose legal-specific AI tools trained on legal documents
  • Configure the AI for your specific jurisdiction's laws
  • Train custom models on your firm's preferred language
  • Build verification into your workflow, not as an afterthought
