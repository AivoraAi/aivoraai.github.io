The Core Question: Does SEOJuice AI Show Spam Score?

Let’s address the elephant in the room. When SEOs ask, "Does it show spam score?", they are usually referring to the famous 1-100% metric popularized by Moz.

Here is the technical truth: SEOJuice AI does not output a metric explicitly labeled "Moz Spam Score." Moz’s Spam Score is a proprietary, trademarked metric that only Moz can legally use.

However, here is the practical reality: SEOJuice AI goes far beyond a simple 1-100% score. Instead, it uses a multi-layered AI approach to calculate what it calls the "Toxicity Index" and "Link Risk Score."

💡 Key Distinction: While Moz’s Spam Score relies heavily on domain-level traits (like domain length, TLD, and historical data), SEOJuice AI uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) to read the actual content on the linking page. It doesn't just tell you the domain is spammy; it tells you why the content is spammy.

So, if you are looking for a tool that identifies spam, flags toxic links, and protects your site, SEOJuice AI absolutely delivers. It just uses a more advanced, AI-driven methodology than the legacy tools of the past.

How SEOJuice AI Detects Spammy Backlinks

To understand if the tool is reliable, you need to understand the engine under the hood. How does SEOJuice AI actually decide if a backlink is toxic? It uses a combination of four powerful AI checks.

1. Contextual Content Analysis (NLP)

Legacy tools look at the domain name. SEOJuice AI reads the page. Using advanced Natural Language Processing, the AI scans the text on the linking page. If the page is filled with spun content, irrelevant keyword stuffing, or foreign language text on a supposedly local site, the AI flags it as highly toxic.

2. Anchor Text Distribution Patterns

Spammy link networks often use exact-match, money-keyword anchor text (e.g., "best cheap insurance"). SEOJuice AI analyzes your entire anchor text profile. If it detects an unnatural spike in exact-match anchors coming from low-authority domains, it immediately raises your Link Risk Score.

3. Link Neighborhood & Trust Flow

Guilty by association is real in SEO. SEOJuice AI maps the "neighborhood" of the linking domain. If your backlink comes from a site that also links to known gambling, pharmaceutical, or adult spam networks, the AI severs the trust connection and marks the link as toxic.

4. Traffic & Engagement Verification

A high DA site with zero organic traffic is a massive red flag. SEOJuice AI cross-references the linking domain with traffic data. If a site claims to have high authority but generates zero real human traffic, the AI assumes it’s a PBN (Private Blog Network) and flags it for disavowal.

Step-by-Step: How to Audit Your Spam Score in SEOJuice AI

Knowing that the tool works is one thing; knowing how to use it is another. Here is the exact workflow I use to clean up toxic backlinks using SEOJuice AI.

Step 1: Initiate the Deep Crawl

Enter your domain URL into the SEOJuice AI dashboard and select "Comprehensive Backlink Audit." Unlike basic checkers that only look at the first 100 links, this instructs the AI to crawl your entire historical backlink profile, including lost and broken links.

Step 2: Review the Toxicity Index

Once the crawl is complete, navigate to the "Health & Safety" tab. Here, you will see your overall Toxicity Index. If it’s above 15%, you have a problem. The AI will provide a visual breakdown of why your score is high (e.g., "40% of links from low-traffic domains").

Step 3: Filter by "High Risk"

Use the filtering tools to isolate links with a "Risk Score" above 70%. These are the critical links that are actively harming your SEO. The AI will show you the exact reason each link was flagged (e.g., "Spun Content Detected" or "Link Farm Neighborhood").

Step 4: The Human Verification

AI is smart, but it’s not infallible. Quickly click through the flagged URLs. If you see a legitimate news site that happens to have a weird domain extension, uncheck it. If you see a directory full of pharmaceutical ads, leave it checked. This step ensures you don't accidentally disavow good links.

Step 5: Export and Disavow

Click "Export to Disavow Format." SEOJuice AI automatically formats the list into a .txt file that meets Google’s strict requirements. Upload this file to Google Search Console, and you have officially protected your site from those toxic links.

Top Alternatives for Checking Spam Scores

If you decide SEOJuice AI isn't the right fit for your workflow, here are the top alternatives you should consider.

1. Moz Link Explorer (The Original)

If you want the classic 1-100% Spam Score, Moz is still the king. It’s based on machine learning models trained on millions of penalized domains. It’s excellent for a quick, high-level overview of your domain’s health. If you are wondering whether Search Atlas AI is free for similar checks, Moz remains the gold standard for free, baseline spam metrics.

2. Semrush Backlink Audit

Semrush offers a highly detailed "Toxicity Score" that integrates directly with the Google Disavow Tool. It is incredibly powerful but comes with a premium price tag. It’s best suited for large agencies managing multiple client portfolios.

3. Ahrefs (Manual Review)

Ahrefs doesn't have an automated "Spam Score" metric. Instead, it gives you the most comprehensive raw data on the web. You have to manually filter by low DR (Domain Rating) and suspicious anchor text. It’s powerful, but it requires a lot of manual elbow grease.
