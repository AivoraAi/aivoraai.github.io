The Secret to Perfect Indian Pronunciations

Here is the biggest challenge with AI TTS: if you type "Bangalore," the AI might say "Bang-lore." If you type "Jugaad," it might say "Joo-gaad" with a hard American 'a'. To get that free AI text to speech natural Indian English sound, you have to trick the AI using phonetic spelling.

🎧 The Phonetic Spelling Cheat Sheet

Write what it sounds like, not how it's spelled!

Common Indian Words & How to Spell Them for AI

  • Namaste: Write "Nu-mus-te" or "Num-us-tey"
  • Jugaad: Write "Joo-gaad" (with a soft 'a' like in 'car')
  • Bengaluru: Write "Ben-guh-loo-roo"
  • Chennai: Write "Chen-nye" (soft 'n' and 'y' sound)
  • Paan: Write "Paa-n" (elongate the 'a')
  • Dhanyavad: Write "Dhun-ya-vah-d"

⚡ Pro Tip: Use hyphens to separate syllables. AI reads hyphens as slight pauses, which helps it articulate complex Indian words much more clearly. Example: "Mah-a-tma G-an-dhi" instead of "Mahatma Gandhi".

Step-by-Step: Generate Your First Indian English Voiceover

Let’s walk through the exact process using Clipchamp, since it offers the best combination of free unlimited access and native Indian English neural voices.

Prepare Your Script

Open a notepad and write your script. Apply the phonetic spelling hacks mentioned above for any local words, names, or cities. Add commas where you want the AI to take a natural breath.

Open Clipchamp & Select TTS

Go to clipchamp.com and log in with a free Microsoft account. Create a new video. On the left sidebar, click on "Record & create" and select "Text to speech."

Choose the Indian English Voice

In the language dropdown, select English (India). You will see voices like "Neerja" and "Prabhat." Neerja is fantastic for educational and lifestyle content, while Prabhat is great for authoritative or tech content.

Fine-Tune Speed and Pitch

AI voices often speak slightly too slowly for modern content. Increase the speaking rate to 1.1x or 1.15x. This instantly makes the voice sound more energetic and human-like. Keep the pitch at default unless you specifically need a deeper or higher voice.

Export and Extract Audio

Add the generated speech to your timeline. If you just want the audio, you can export the project as an MP4 and use a free tool like CloudConvert or an online MP4 to MP3 converter to extract the audio file. Alternatively, Clipchamp now allows direct audio exports in some regions.

Advanced Hacks for Ultra-Realistic Audio

Want to take your voiceovers from "good" to "indistinguishable from a human"? Here are the advanced techniques I use.

1. The Power of Punctuation

AI reads punctuation as instructions.
• Use commas for short pauses.
• Use ellipses (...) for thoughtful, longer pauses.
• Use exclamation marks to increase energy and pitch slightly.
• Use quotation marks if the AI is reading dialogue; it often changes the intonation to sound like someone is speaking.

2. SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language)

If you use Clipchamp or Azure directly, you can use SSML tags to control the audio at a microscopic level.

<speak> Welcome to Aivora AI. <break time="500ms"/> Today, we are exploring the best <emphasis level="strong">free AI text to speech</emphasis> tools for Indian English. <break time="300ms"/> Let's dive in! </speak>

3. Background Noise & Room Tone

Pure AI audio is too clean; it sounds artificial. To make it natural, add a very subtle layer of "room tone" or low-frequency background noise in your video editor. Lower the volume of the noise to -30dB. It tricks the brain into thinking the voice was recorded in a real physical space.

Writing Scripts That Sound Human

The best AI voice in the world will sound robotic if you feed it a poorly written script. AI reads exactly what you write. If you write like an academic textbook, it will sound like a textbook. If you write like you talk, it will sound like a human.

When you sit down to write your TTS script, follow these rules:

  1. Use contractions: Write "It's" instead of "It is". Write "You'll" instead of "You will".
  2. Keep sentences short: AI struggles with long, winding sentences with multiple clauses. Keep it punchy.
  3. Add conversational filler: Occasionally add "So," "Well," or "You know," to make it feel unscripted.
