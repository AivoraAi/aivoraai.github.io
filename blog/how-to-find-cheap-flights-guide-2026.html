15 Proven Strategies to Find Cheap Flights

📅 1. Book at the Right Time

The Sweet Spot: For domestic flights, book 1-3 months in advance. For international, book 2-8 months ahead.

Best Day to Book: Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons (when airlines release deals).

Best Day to Fly: Tuesday, Wednesday, and Saturday (less demand = lower prices).

💰 Average Savings: $100-300

🔍 2. Use Incognito/Private Browsing

Airlines and booking sites track your searches and may raise prices if they see repeated searches for the same route.

How to do it: Always search in incognito/private mode to avoid dynamic pricing based on your browsing history.

💰 Average Savings: $50-150

🌍 3. Be Flexible with Dates

Flying just 1-2 days earlier or later can save you hundreds. Use flexible date search tools to compare prices across a month.

Pro tip: Mid-week flights (Tue-Thu) are typically 20-30% cheaper than weekend flights.

💰 Average Savings: $150-400

🛫 4. Try Nearby Airports

Flying into/out of alternative airports can dramatically reduce costs. Example: Flying into Bergamo instead of Milan, or Burbank instead of LAX.

Tool: Use Google Flights' "Nearby airports" feature to compare all options.

💰 Average Savings: $100-350

🔄 5. Use Flight Search Engines

Don't rely on just one site. Compare prices across multiple platforms:

  • Google Flights - Best for flexible date searches
  • Skyscanner - Great for "everywhere" searches
  • Kayak - Price alerts and flexible calendars
  • Momondo - Often finds cheaper combinations
  • Klook - Exclusive deals and packages
💰 Average Savings: $80-250

⏰ 6. Set Price Alerts

Let deals come to you! Set up price alerts on Google Flights, Kayak, or Skyscanner for your desired route. You'll get notifications when prices drop.

Best for: Planning trips 3-6 months in advance.

💰 Average Savings: $100-300

🎒 7. Consider Budget Airlines

Low-cost carriers like Ryanair, EasyJet, Spirit, and Southwest can be 40-60% cheaper than major airlines.

Warning: Watch for hidden fees (bags, seat selection, printing boarding passes).

💰 Average Savings: $150-500

🎁 8. Use Travel Rewards & Points

Maximize credit card rewards, airline miles, and loyalty programs. Many cards offer 50,000-100,000 point sign-up bonuses (worth $500-1,000+ in flights).

Top cards: Chase Sapphire, Amex Gold, Capital One Venture.

💰 Average Savings: $300-1,000+

🌐 9. Try Hidden City Ticketing

Sometimes booking a flight with a layover in your actual destination is cheaper than a direct flight. (Example: NYC to Chicago via Denver might be cheaper than NYC to Denver direct).

Tool: Use Skiplagged.com to find these deals.

Warning: Only works for one-way tickets and you can't check bags.

💰 Average Savings: $100-400

📱 10. Book Last-Minute (Sometimes)

Airlines sometimes slash prices 1-2 weeks before departure to fill empty seats. Apps like HotelTonight and Priceline's "Express Deals" offer deep discounts.

Best for: Flexible travelers with no set dates.

💰 Average Savings: $150-500

🎓 11. Use Student/Youth Discounts

If you're under 26 or a student, you qualify for special fares through StudentUniverse, STA Travel, and airline youth programs.

💰 Average Savings: $50-200

🔄 12. Try Multi-City or Open-Jaw Tickets

Flying into one city and out of another (open-jaw) or adding a stopover can sometimes be cheaper than round-trip to one destination.

Example: Fly into Paris, out of Rome — often cheaper than Paris round-trip.

💰 Average Savings: $100-300

🎯 13. Clear Your Cookies

Similar to incognito mode, clearing cookies prevents airlines from tracking your searches and potentially raising prices.

💰 Average Savings: $30-100

📧 14. Subscribe to Deal Newsletters

Get flash sale alerts from:

  • Scott's Cheap Flights (now Going.com)
  • The Flight Deal
  • Secret Flying
  • Airfarewatchdog
💰 Average Savings: $200-600

🎪 15. Book Package Deals

Sometimes booking flight + hotel together saves money. Check Expedia, Priceline, and Klook for bundle deals.

💰 Average Savings: $150-400

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake #1: Booking Too Early or Too Late
Too early (10+ months) = high prices. Too late (last week) = panic prices. Solution: Book 1-3 months ahead for domestic, 2-8 months for international.

❌ Mistake #2: Only Checking One Airport
Limiting yourself to one airport can cost you hundreds. Solution: Always check nearby airports (within 100 miles).

❌ Mistake #3: Ignoring Budget Airlines
Major airlines aren't always cheapest. Solution: Always compare budget carriers, but factor in baggage fees.

❌ Mistake #4: Not Using Incognito Mode
Airlines track searches and may raise prices. Solution: Always search in private/incognito browsing.
