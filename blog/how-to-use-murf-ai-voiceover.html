Quick Answer

To create a voiceover with Murf AI: Sign up free at murf.ai → create a new project → type your script → choose a voice → adjust speed and pitch → click Export. The entire process takes under 10 minutes for a typical 2-3 minute voiceover.

Creating a professional voiceover used to mean renting a studio, hiring a voice actor, or recording yourself a dozen times until it sounded right.

With Murf AI, the process takes under 10 minutes — from blank script to polished audio file. Here's the exact step-by-step process, including pro tips at every stage that most tutorials skip.

Before You Start — What You Need

  • A Murf AI account (free — no credit card required)
  • Your script written out in advance (Murf works best when your text is ready)
  • A paid plan if you need to download the audio file (free plan = preview only)

⭐ Pro tip before you start: Write your script in short, punchy sentences. Murf AI handles short sentences much more naturally than long, complex ones. Aim for sentences under 20 words each.

Step-by-Step: Create a Voiceover in Murf AI

1

Sign Up for a Free Account

Go to murf.ai and click "Get Started Free." Sign up with Google or email. No credit card required. You land directly in the Murf Studio dashboard.

💡 Use your Google account — it's the fastest sign-up method
2

Create a New Project

Click "+ New Project" in the top left. Name your project (e.g. "YouTube Intro Voiceover"). Choose between Voice Studio, Voice Over Video, or Dubbing based on your goal.

💡 Choose "Voice Studio" for audio-only, "Voice Over Video" if you have a video file
3

Type or Paste Your Script

Click on the text block and type or paste your script. Each block handles up to 3,000 characters. For longer scripts, add multiple blocks by pressing Enter and clicking the + button.

💡 Break your script into sections — intro, body, conclusion — using separate blocks for easier editing
4

Choose Your Voice

Click the voice selector on the left panel. Browse by language, gender, age, and style. Click any voice to hear it read your exact text — not a pre-recorded sample. Test at least 3-5 voices before deciding.

💡 Top voice picks: "Marcus" for professional, "Natalie" for warm, "Miles" for conversational YouTube style
5

Fine-Tune the Delivery

Use the controls on the right panel to adjust: Speed (0.5x to 2x), Pitch (-50 to +50), and Volume. For word-level control, highlight any word and click "Emphasis" to stress it, or insert a pause between words using the Pause button.

💡 Most professional narration works best at 0.95x speed — slightly slower than the default
6

Fix Mispronunciations

If Murf mispronounces a word (brand names, technical terms), highlight it and click "Pronunciation". Type the phonetic spelling (e.g. "Aivora" → "eye-VOR-ah") and save. This fixes it globally across the project.

💡 Always do a full playback before exporting to catch pronunciation issues
7

Add Background Music (Optional)

Click "Background Music" in the left panel. Browse the royalty-free library by mood (Corporate, Inspiring, Relaxed, etc.) and adjust the music volume relative to your voiceover. This step is Pro plan only.

💡 Keep background music at 10-20% volume — subtle underscore, not competing with narration
8

Export Your Voiceover

Click the "Export" button in the top right. Choose your format: MP3 (smaller file, web use), WAV (highest quality, for video editing), or export directly as a video file if you used the video sync feature.

💡 Use WAV for video editing — it preserves full audio quality for post-processing

✅ Total time: For a typical 2-3 minute script, the full process — typing, voice selection, fine-tuning, and export — takes approximately 8-12 minutes on your first try. By your third project, you'll be under 5 minutes.

7 Pro Tips for Better Murf AI Voiceovers

Tip 1 — Always preview on your actual script. Murf's voice previews read your real text, not a stock sample. This means the voice you choose sounds exactly as it will in the final export.

Tip 2 — Use punctuation to control pacing. Commas create short pauses. Periods create longer pauses. Em dashes (—) create a dramatic beat. Use them intentionally in your script.

Tip 3 — Don't use ALL CAPS for emphasis. Use the built-in Emphasis button instead. ALL CAPS often makes Murf shout the word unnaturally.

Tip 4 — Test multiple voices on the same paragraph. The same sentence can sound completely different in two voices. Always compare at least 3 before committing.

Tip 5 — Split long scripts into sections. Multiple blocks let you apply different voices or speeds to different parts — great for interview-style content or dialogue.

Tip 6 — Export as WAV for video editing. MP3 is fine for web delivery, but WAV gives your video editor more headroom for audio processing.

Tip 7 — Save pronunciation fixes as a custom dictionary. On Pro plans, you can save your pronunciation library so you never have to reteach brand names in new projects.

How to Sync Murf AI Voiceover to a Video

This is one of Murf AI's most powerful features — and most creators don't use it:

  1. Create a new project and choose "Voice Over Video".
  2. Upload your video file (MP4, MOV, AVI supported).
  3. The video appears on a timeline at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Add text blocks that align to specific timestamps on the timeline.
  5. Play back the video with the voiceover synced — adjust timing as needed.
  6. Export as a complete video file with voiceover baked in.

⚠️ Note: Video sync is a Pro plan feature only. It is not available on the free or Basic plans.
