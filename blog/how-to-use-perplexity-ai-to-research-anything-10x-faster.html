⚙️ Getting Started: Setup and First Steps

Go to perplexity.ai and sign up with email or Google. The free tier includes unlimited queries, source citations, and basic AI models—more than enough to get started.

Step 1: Understand the Interface

  • Search bar: Type your question naturally, like you would ask a person.
  • Response panel: Shows the AI's answer with numbered citations.
  • Source panel: Lists all referenced articles with titles and URLs.

Step 2: Ask Your First Question

Start simple. Try: "What are the latest developments in renewable energy storage in 2026?" Notice how the answer includes specific studies and policy changes—all with clickable sources.

🚀 Advanced Techniques for 10x Faster Research

Technique 1: Master Prompt Engineering

The quality of your answer depends heavily on how you ask.

  • Bad prompt: "Tell me about climate change."
  • Good prompt: "Summarize key findings from IPCC reports published in 2024-2026 on climate adaptation for coastal cities, focusing on cost-effective solutions."

Technique 2: Use Focus Modes Strategically

Perplexity offers "Focus" modes that tailor its search behavior:

  • Academic: Prioritizes peer-reviewed papers and scholarly sources.
  • Writing: Optimizes for content creation and drafting assistance.
  • Math: Enhances numerical reasoning and formula handling.
  • YouTube/Reddit: Searches video transcripts or community discussions.

If you are an educator building lesson plans, combining Perplexity's Academic focus with the best AI tools for teachers and educators can completely transform your classroom preparation.

Technique 3: Chain Your Questions

Research is iterative. Don't ask one question and stop. Use follow-ups to dive deeper:

  1. "What are the main causes of urban heat islands?"
  2. "Which cities have implemented successful mitigation strategies?"
  3. "What was the cost-benefit analysis of Singapore's green roof program?"

⚠️ 3 Mistakes That Kill AI Research

1. Treating Answers as Final Truth: Perplexity synthesizes information, but it can still make errors. Always verify critical claims, especially for academic or professional work.

2. Using Vague Prompts: "Tell me about AI" will get you a generic overview. Specificity regarding timeframe, source type, and audience is your superpower.

3. Ignoring Source Quality: Just because Perplexity cites a source doesn't mean it's credible. A blog post from an unknown website carries less weight than a peer-reviewed journal.
