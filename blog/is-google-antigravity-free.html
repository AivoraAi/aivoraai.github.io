Is Google Antigravity Free?
Complete Pricing & Free Tier Breakdown

Published: June 13, 2026 10 min read By Varun Lalwani

Everyone wants to know: can you use Google's powerful AI coding agent for free? We break down the free tier limits, hidden costs, and whether you actually need to pay in 2026.

Quick Answer

Yes, Google Antigravity offers a free tier with approximately 50-100 agent runs per month, perfect for students, hobbyists, and experimentation. However, the free tier has limitations: slower processing speeds, limited context window (100K tokens), no access to premium Gemini Ultra models, and community-only support. For professional development with unlimited agent runs and advanced features, paid plans start at $20/month. No credit card required to start the free tier.

The Truth About Google Antigravity's Free Tier

When Google announced Antigravity in late 2025, the developer community erupted with one burning question: "Will it be free?" After all, GitHub Copilot started as a paid-only tool, and many developers were hoping Google would disrupt the market with a genuinely free alternative to AI-assisted coding.

The answer is both yes and no. Google Antigravity does offer a free tier, but it's not unlimited. Understanding exactly what you get for free — and where the paywalls kick in — is crucial before you invest time learning the platform.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down every aspect of Google Antigravity's pricing, compare the free tier against paid plans, and help you decide whether you can get by with the free version or if you'll need to upgrade for your specific use case.

What's Included in the Free Tier?

Google's free tier is surprisingly generous for individual developers and students. Here's exactly what you get without paying a dime:

Monthly Credits

50-100 Agent Runs

Enough for small projects, learning, and experimentation. One "agent run" equals one autonomous coding task from prompt to completion.

Context Window

100K Tokens

Can process approximately 75,000 words or a medium-sized codebase. Sufficient for most single-file or small multi-file projects.

AI Models

Gemini Pro Access

Access to the standard Gemini Pro model, which handles most coding tasks effectively. No access to Ultra models.

Access to Google's community forums and documentation. No direct email or chat support for free tier users.

Hidden Costs & Gotchas to Watch For

While the pricing seems straightforward, there are a few things Google doesn't advertise prominently:

⚠️ Credit Rollover: Unused free tier credits do NOT roll over to the next month. Use them or lose them.

💸 Overage Charges: If you exceed your free tier limit, you'll be charged $0.10 per additional agent run unless you upgrade to Pro.

✅ No Credit Card Required: Unlike many competitors, you don't need to provide payment information to start the free tier.

Final Verdict: Is the Free Tier Worth It?

After extensive testing, here's our honest take:

The free tier is genuinely useful — not just a crippled trial. If you're a student, hobbyist, or solo developer working on small projects, you can absolutely build real applications without paying a dime. The 50-100 monthly credits are enough to create 2-3 substantial features per week.

However, if you're doing professional development work, the Pro plan at $20/month is a no-brainer. That's less than the cost of two coffees for unlimited autonomous AI coding agents. The productivity gains alone will pay for the subscription in hours.
