3 Master Prompts for Kimi AI E-E-A-T Content

Ready to implement? Here are the exact prompts we use at Aivora AI to generate high-E-E-A-T content using Kimi.

1
The "Experience Weaver" Prompt
Best for: Adding first-hand experience to dry topics

"I am going to provide you with two documents. Document A contains my raw, personal notes, failures, and successes from [Topic]. Document B contains authoritative, factual data about [Topic].

Your Task: Write a 2,000-word guide. You must use the factual data from Document B to ensure accuracy, but you must structure the narrative around the personal experiences in Document A.

Rules:

1. Use first-person perspective ('When I tried...', 'I discovered that...').

2. Explicitly mention failures and mistakes from Document A to build authenticity.

3. Do not use generic AI transitions like 'In conclusion' or 'It is important to note'."

2
The "Authority Synthesizer" Prompt
Best for: YMYL topics requiring deep expertise

"Analyze the 15 attached academic/industry reports. Identify the top 3 areas where these experts disagree.

Your Task: Write an objective analysis of these conflicting viewpoints. Do not take a side. Instead, explain the methodology behind each side's argument.

Requirement: Every claim must be followed by a citation in the format [Report Name, Page X]. If a fact is not in the provided documents, do not include it."

3
The "Trust Auditor" Prompt
Best for: Reviewing existing content for E-E-A-T gaps

"Act as a strict Google Search Quality Rater. Review the attached article draft against the E-E-A-T guidelines.

Identify:

1. Where does the content lack first-hand experience?

2. Where are the claims unsupported by evidence?

3. Where is the tone overly promotional rather than objective?

Output: A table of specific paragraphs that need rewriting to improve Trustworthiness, with suggested fixes."

Common E-E-A-T Mistakes with Kimi AI

Even with Kimi's power, creators make critical errors that destroy E-E-A-T.

1. The "Frankenstein" Context Dump

Uploading 50 unrelated documents and asking Kimi to "write a good article." Kimi will try to connect everything, resulting in a confused, unfocused piece. Always curate your context window. Upload only the most relevant 5-10 sources and your specific notes.

2. Hiding the AI

Trustworthiness requires transparency. Do not pretend AI-written content is 100% human-generated. Add a disclosure: "This guide was researched using Kimi AI and verified by our editorial team." Paradoxically, admitting AI assistance often increases trust because it shows honesty.

3. Ignoring the "So What?" Factor

Kimi can summarize data perfectly, but it doesn't know why your audience cares. You must manually inject the "So what?"—the practical implication of the data. If Kimi writes "The market grew by 5%," you must add, "This means your Q3 budget needs to account for increased ad costs."

Warning: Never use Kimi to generate medical, legal, or financial advice without explicit, rigorous human expert review. The "Trustworthiness" pillar for YMYL topics requires absolute accuracy, and AI hallucinations in these niches can have severe real-world consequences.
