What Is Lindy AI?

Lindy AI is a no-code platform for building AI agents that automate business tasks across thousands of apps — Gmail, Slack, Google Calendar, Notion, HubSpot, Salesforce, and more — using natural language instructions instead of drag-and-drop workflow builders.

Where traditional automation tools like Zapier follow rigid "when X happens, do Y" logic, a Lindy agent can reason about context: it reads an email, understands what's being asked, drafts a reply in your tone, and only loops you in when it's genuinely unsure. Multiple agents can be chained together to handle multi-step processes end to end.

Lindy AI vs Zapier: Which Should You Use?

This is the comparison most people searching for Lindy actually want answered, so here's the honest breakdown:

💡 Use Zapier or Make for simple, predictable "when X happens, do Y" automations — they're often cheaper for high-volume, rule-based triggers with no judgment involved.

⚠️ Use Lindy AI for tasks that need context and judgment — triaging emails, summarising meetings, qualifying leads, or drafting replies in your voice. Many teams run both tools side by side rather than choosing one.

Our Final Verdict

🤖 Editor's Pick for 2026

Lindy AI earns its place as one of the most approachable AI agent builders we've tested in 2026. The free plan is genuinely usable, the natural-language setup removes the steepest part of the learning curve, and the agents handle context-heavy tasks — email, meetings, lead qualification — better than rule-based tools.

You describe a task in plain English, such as "read my inbox every morning and draft replies to client emails in my tone." Lindy interprets that instruction, connects to the relevant apps like Gmail, Slack, or your CRM, and builds an AI agent (called a "Lindy") that runs on a trigger you define. The agent can reason about context, make decisions, and escalate to a human when it's unsure, rather than following a rigid if-then sequence.

Lindy AI and Zapier solve different problems. Zapier and Make excel at simple, predictable "when X happens, do Y" automations and are often cheaper for high-volume simple triggers. Lindy is better suited to tasks that require judgment, such as triaging emails, summarising meetings, or qualifying leads, because its agents can reason about context instead of just executing fixed steps.
