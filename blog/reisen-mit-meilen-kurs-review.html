Quick Answer

Reisen mit Meilen Kurs ("Travel with Miles Course") is a comprehensive German-language program teaching travel hacking: earning airline miles, maximizing credit card points, and booking premium flights for up to 90% off. While its credit card recommendations are heavily focused on the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland), the core strategies regarding airline alliances (Star Alliance, Oneworld, SkyTeam) and award search methodologies are universally applicable. If you can save €1,500 on a single business class ticket using these methods, the course pays for itself multiple times over.

Travel hacking — the art of using airline miles, credit card points, and loyalty programs to travel for less — has exploded in popularity. But let's be honest: most of the best resources are in English, leaving German-speaking travelers (and non-native speakers wanting German instruction) completely underserved.

Enter Reisen mit Meilen Kurs: A digital training program promising to teach you how to earn miles, maximize points, and book premium travel for a fraction of the cash cost.

But does it actually deliver? Is it worth the investment? And can you use these strategies if you live outside Germany, Austria, or Switzerland?

I've analyzed the curriculum, tested the strategies, and broken down everything you need to know about Reisen mit Meilen Kurs — so you can decide if it's the right fit for your travel goals in 2026.

🎯 Who Is This Course For?

✅ Perfect For: German-speaking travelers wanting to travel more for less · Expats in the DACH region · Points beginners needing structured guidance · Frequent flyers wanting to maximize existing loyalty accounts.

❌ Not Ideal For: Travelers exclusively using non-alliance airlines · Those seeking advanced award chart arbitrage · People outside Europe needing highly specific local card recommendations (though the framework still applies).

✈️ Best For: DACH region travelers & German speakers

✅ Pros of Reisen mit Meilen Kurs

  • 🇩🇪 German-Language Instruction: Fills a major gap — most travel hacking content is English-only. Perfect for native speakers or those learning German.
  • 📚 Structured Curriculum: Step-by-step modules take you from beginner to confident award booker — no guesswork required.
  • 💶 Region-Specific Advice: Focuses on credit cards, airlines, and programs available in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland — crucial for practical application.
  • 🔄 Evergreen Strategies: Core principles of travel hacking (alliances, transfer partners, sweet spots) remain relevant even as specific programs change.
  • 💰 High ROI Potential: One well-booked business class flight can save €1,000–€3,000 — easily covering the course cost many times over.

❌ Cons of Reisen mit Meilen Kurs

  • ⚠️ Regional Focus: Strategies optimized for the DACH region may not directly apply to travelers in India, Southeast Asia, or the Americas without some adaptation.
  • 🔄 Program Changes: Airline loyalty programs change frequently. Course content requires periodic updates to stay current (though lifetime updates are included).
  • 💳 Credit Requirements: Many high-value cards require good credit history — a barrier for some beginners or those in countries with different credit systems.
  • 🌐 Language Barrier: If you don't speak German, the course won't be accessible (though this is by design).

⭐ Honest Verdict: Reisen mit Meilen Kurs is a legitimate, well-structured program that delivers real value for German-speaking travelers. It won't magically book your flights for you, but it provides the knowledge framework to save thousands on travel over time. Best used as a foundation — then adapt strategies to your specific region and travel patterns.

🌍 Can You Use This Course Outside German-Speaking Countries?

Yes — but with adaptation. Here's how travelers worldwide can benefit:

  • 🇮🇳 India / 🇵🇭 Philippines / 🌍 Africa: Core concepts (alliances, transfer partners, award search) are universal. Use the framework, then research local credit cards and airline partners. Focus on programs with global reach: Star Alliance (Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines), Oneworld (British Airways, Qatar), SkyTeam (Air France, Delta).
  • 🇺🇸 USA / 🇬🇧 UK / 🇦🇺 Australia: Many strategies transfer directly. Supplement with region-specific resources (e.g., The Points Guy for US cards). The course's award booking methodology is globally applicable.

🔑 Key Insight: Travel hacking principles are universal. The course teaches the how; you apply it to your region's what (cards, airlines, programs).
